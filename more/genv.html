Flipping the Table

Pokémon Black and White is the first title to accumulate less sales than its prior installement, while still being a commercial success. In comparison with Diamond and Pearl, the prior generation, Pokémon Black and White hit around 2 million less sales. With many fans citing Pokémon Black and White as their favorite generation, it is questionable why this game did worse than its predecessors. With innovations in story, design, gameplay, and more, this installment had interestingly become perhaps both the most forgettable yet most memorable mattering on who you'd ask. This comes down to a difference in previously core features of the Pokémon series, which is a similar reason for the outrage seen in the Gen VIII controversy.

The criticisms in Pokémon Black and White came down to a lack of nostalgic air, the game having decided to not include previous Pokémon until the post-game. Additionally, Black and White attempted to be so ground-breaking that it introduced a plethora of changes, in region, battling, visuals, in depth storytelling, and so forth. The amount of changes had become unwelcome to returning fans who had wished for a similar, nostalgic adventure that did not reach so far in grasping new audiences. This is where its controversy comes in, as while many fans came to appreciate this game as the most innovative, with more focus on storytelling than any of its predecessors or successors, more new Pokémon and a different take on setting, this also became its downfall for those who had become attached to the simularity of those old features.
